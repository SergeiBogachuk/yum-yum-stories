How pricing works

Most jobs should be quoted as owner loading service because that is where the convenience and margin are. Drop-off only stays available for the right customer, but the main offer is that you show up, load the debris, and haul it out. Weight still matters on both options.

FAQ

What customers usually ask before booking.

What if I do not know the exact weight?

That is normal. Use the calculator as a quick estimate, then text a few photos for a tighter quote. The final weight is confirmed after disposal.

What is the main service you offer?

The main service is owner loading with the dump trailer. In other words, you show up, load the debris, and haul it away. Trailer drop-off only is still available for some jobs, but it is not the main pitch.

When do overage charges apply?

Only when the actual dump weight is above the included weight limit for the debris type you booked. If it stays under the included limit, there is no overage charge.

Can I pay by Zelle?

Yes, Zelle can be your preferred payment method for deposits and balances if your business bank account supports it. Card or invoice payment can still be offered when a customer wants a formal payment link.

Does Zelle mean I do not have to deal with taxes?

No. Payment method and tax responsibility are different things. Zelle may help reduce processing costs, but business income still has to be tracked and reported properly.

Why is owner loading service more expensive?

Because that changes the job from simple trailer rental into labor service. Once loading is included, the estimate also has to cover time, lifting, carrying distance, stairs, and job difficulty.

Which materials usually cost more?

Shingles, concrete, dirt, brick, and tile are much heavier than normal household junk, so they often have lower included tonnage and a higher overage rate.

Can I just text instead of filling everything out?

Yes. If it is easier, text the address and a few photos to dispatch and you can get the conversation moving that way.
